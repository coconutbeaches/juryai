My name is Maya Chen and I run a small consulting business. In April I hired Alex Rivera to build a five-page website for $2,400. I paid the $1,200 deposit immediately — that part is not in dispute, and neither is the basic scope: homepage, about, services, contact, mobile.

What is in dispute is almost everything about how it went.

The May 20 launch date was not an “estimate” to me. I told Alex from the first call that I was running a paid marketing push in late May and the site had to be live before it started. On May 12 I emailed him asking point blank whether we were still on track for May 20, and he replied “yes, we’re on track.” I have that email. If the date was flexible in his mind, that was the moment to say so.

Alex says my content was late. Some of it was — I’ll be honest about that. The contract said April 25 and I sent most of the copy around April 26 or 27, I think, definitely before the end of the month. My assistant sent the last batch of photos later, around May 8, which I only realized when I went back through the messages. So I was part of the early delay. But a few days of late photos does not explain delivering the first full version on June 3, two weeks after the launch date he had confirmed on May 12.

On the “extra” requests: the pricing comparison section wasn’t an addition, it replaced content on the services page, and Alex told me on WhatsApp that the changes were included. I have a screenshot of him saying “no problem, that’s included.” I no longer have the full conversation — I cleared old chats when my phone storage filled up — but the screenshot is real.

The June 3 version looked fine on desktop, and yes, I told him it “looked really good overall.” On my phone it was a different story. The menu didn’t open on mobile and two pages overlapped so badly you couldn’t read them. I sent Alex a screen recording on June 5, and he replied — I have this message — “you’re right, mobile is a mess right now, give me a few days.” His own June 8 recording just shows it working on his phone.

Then he essentially vanished. Between roughly June 9 and June 22 I got two short messages. He mentioned a family issue and I’m sympathetic, but I had a campaign booked. I ended up pushing the launch entirely and I estimate the delay cost me at least three to four thousand dollars in lost client bookings, since May and June are my peak inquiry months.

I’ll also admit: I did use two of the design images in Instagram posts in early June, and the site was briefly reachable at my domain for a few days while we were testing. I wasn’t hiding a finished website from him. It was a partial, broken site that I couldn’t run a campaign on.

I don’t think I should pay $1,200 for a project delivered late, incomplete on mobile, and after his own written confirmation of the deadline. I’m willing to pay $600 to close this out, in exchange for the source files and admin credentials, because I did receive usable design work. If Alex won’t accept that, I want $400 of my deposit back and we both walk away.
